What is the central angle of a circle?
The central angle of a circle is the angle whose apex is the center O of a circle and whose legs are radii intersecting the circle in two distinct points A and B. Central angles are subtended by an arc between those two points. And the arc length is the central angle of a circle of radius one.
How to calculate central angle of a circle?
To calculate central angle of a circle you should use the following formula:
Φ = s / r
where:
Φ = central angle
s = arc length
r = radius length
Why use central angle of a circle?
The central angle of a circle is used in order to divide a circle into sectors.
